## Why it matters

![Coding Interview Tips](../../src/images/06_dsa/dsa-1c.png)

<details>
  <summary>Why did tech companies shift from brain teasers to coding interviews</summary>
  <div>
    <p>Brain teasers failed to assess true programming skills and experience. Coding interviews offer a more inclusive way to evaluate problem-solving and technical ability.</p>
  </div>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>What do interviewers look for during a coding interview</summary>
  <div>
    <p>They assess your approach to problems, coding efficiency, debugging skills, and how clearly you communicate your reasoning.</p>
  </div>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Is it okay to make minor mistakes during a coding interview</summary>
  <div>
    <p>Yes — identifying and fixing bugs on the fly shows strong debugging skills and attention to detail.</p>
  </div>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Why is it important to explain your thought process while coding</summary>
  <div>
    <p>It helps interviewers understand your decisions, trade-offs, and overall problem-solving approach.</p>
  </div>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>How should you choose your coding language for an interview</summary>
  <div>
    <p>Confirm your preferred language with the recruiter beforehand to ensure alignment with the role.</p>
  </div>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Why is it important to understand different coding interview formats</summary>
  <div>
    <p>Each format has unique expectations and challenges — knowing them helps you prepare effectively.</p>
  </div>
</details>

![Coding Interview Formats](../../src/images/06_dsa/dsa-1f.png)

<details>
  <summary>What is the purpose of an online assessment</summary>
  <div>
    <p>Timed remote test with coding challenges or MCQs to evaluate technical skills.</p>
  </div>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>What happens during a phone screen</summary>
  <div>
    <p>30–60 min call to assess role understanding, team fit, and possibly technical skills.</p>
  </div>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>What’s expected in a take-home assignment</summary>
  <div>
    <p>Solve a coding task with clean structure, testing, and documentation at your own pace.</p>
  </div>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>What do final interviews involve</summary>
  <div>
    <p>Series of technical/behavioral rounds with whiteboard or online coding tools.</p>
  </div>
</details>

## Problem-Solving Framework

![Problem-Solving Framework](../../src/images/06_dsa/dsa-1b.png)

<details>
  <summary>What’s the first step when approaching a coding interview problem</summary>
  <div>
    <p>Restate the problem, ask clarifying questions, and gather constraints and edge cases.</p>
  </div>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>How should you begin designing your solution</summary>
  <div>
    <p>Start with a brute-force approach, discuss alternatives, and use pseudocode to outline logic.</p>
  </div>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Why estimate time and space complexity early</summary>
  <div>
    <p>It shows awareness of efficiency and helps guide design decisions.</p>
  </div>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>What makes your code interview-ready</summary>
  <div>
    <p>Use clean structure, meaningful variable names, helper functions, and custom classes if needed.</p>
  </div>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>What should you do after coding</summary>
  <div>
    <p>Reassess and explain final time and space complexity, noting changes from initial estimates.</p>
  </div>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>How should you test your solution</summary>
  <div>
    <p>Use simple cases, edge cases, boundary values, and ensure correctness across valid input ranges.</p>
  </div>
</details>

## Effective Communication in an interview

![Effective Communication](../../src/images/06_dsa/dsa-1a.png)

<details>
  <summary>How does time mismanagement hurt</summary>
   <div><p>You may not finish or show complete thinking.</p></div>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Why is thinking out loud important in interviews</summary>
   <div><p>It helps the interviewer follow your reasoning and see how you tackle problems from different angles.</p></div>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>What should you do to ensure the interviewer understands your approach</summary>
   <div><p>Regularly check in, clarify confusion, and correct misunderstandings to stay aligned.</p>
    <div>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>How should you handle multiple solution paths</summary>
   <div><p>Share your options, compare pros and cons, and explain your final decision.</p>
    <div>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>What should you do if you’re stuck</summary>
   <div><p>Explain your thought process, what you’ve tried, and why you’re stuck—this invites guidance.</p>
    <div>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Is it okay to pause during an interview</summary>
   <div><p>Yes—just let the interviewer know you’re taking a moment to think, so they understand you’re processing.</p>
    <div>
</details>
